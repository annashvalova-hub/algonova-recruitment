Key insights
What the data tells us
01
Weekend shifts is the biggest funnel killer. 118 leads (30% of the entire base) prefer weekends — yet only 2 of them (1.7%) made it past screening. The ad may be attracting people looking for casual side income, not professional tutors.
02
81 leads were never properly followed up — including 45 with 3+ years of teaching experience. These are potentially the strongest candidates in the pipeline, sitting untouched.
03
Experience level barely predicts conversion. Candidates with 1–2 years convert at 22.6% vs 21.7% for 3+ years. A 1-year teacher is just as likely to pass screening as a veteran — seniority alone isn't the filter.
04
Full-time candidates convert 26x better than weekend candidates (44% vs 1.7%). Shifting ad targeting toward people open to flexible or full-time work should dramatically improve lead quality.
05
32 leads were rejected for having no math teaching experience despite answering "Yes" on the form. The screening question isn't filtering effectively — a more specific question or a short test task at sign-up could save significant recruiter time.
06
Bekasi and Palembang punch above their weight — 40% and 43% conversion despite small volumes. These cities may have a stronger pool of qualified tutors relative to applicant count and could be worth targeted investment.
